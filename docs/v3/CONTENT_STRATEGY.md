# Content Strategy

## Homepage narrative

1. Local premium promise and service footprint
2. Primary CTA to request an estimate
3. Factual trust strip
4. Core services
5. How service works
6. What is included and what affects price
7. Why homeowners choose the company
8. Service-area overview
9. Frequently asked questions
10. Final CTA and complete contact/footer information

## Approved core messaging

- Primary headline: `Come Home to Immaculate.`
- Supporting idea: recurring, deep, and move-in/move-out residential cleaning with exceptional care and dependable communication.
- Positioning: locally owned, white-glove service, systems-driven reliability.

## Trust claims

Render claims only when their matching feature flag and evidence reference are configured. Examples:

- Maryland business registration verified
- Insured
- Background-checked team
- Supplies provided
- Satisfaction response policy
- Secure payments
- Verified Google review rating

Safe initial claims include only facts the owner can substantiate, such as locally owned, clear quotes, documented checklists, or supplies provided once operationally true.

## Pricing wording

Show configurable starting prices, not guaranteed universal totals. Always display:

> Prices shown are starting estimates for homes in average condition. Your confirmed quote is based on size, layout, condition, bathrooms, access, frequency, and selected add-ons.

## Local SEO content

Location pages must contain useful local service details, travel/availability context, relevant housing characteristics or service considerations, nearby covered communities, and a unique FAQ—not just swapped place names.

## Reviews

Do not render testimonials until real, permissioned or platform-sourced reviews exist. If reviews are integrated later, comply with platform terms and never add self-authored placeholder reviews.
